\# AI-POWERED INSURANCE FOR INDIA’S GIG ECONOMY



\## Problem Statement



India’s gig economy is growing rapidly, with millions of workers depending on daily or weekly earnings through platforms like food delivery, logistics, ride-sharing, and quick commerce.



These workers are highly vulnerable to external disruptions such as:



\- Heavy rainfall

\- Floods

\- Heatwaves

\- Poor air quality

\- Local curfews

\- Strikes

\- Road closures

\- Sudden zone restrictions



When these events happen, gig workers are often unable to work, which directly affects their income.



Traditional insurance products mainly focus on health, life, vehicle, or accident coverage. However, this problem statement specifically requires protection for \*\*income loss caused by external disruptions\*\*.



\### Our Goal

To build an AI-powered parametric insurance platform that provides fast, automatic, and transparent weekly income protection for gig workers whenever such disruptions happen.





\## Our Solution Overview



We propose GigGuard AI, a smart micro-insurance platform designed specifically for gig workers in India.



GigGuard AI provides:



\- Weekly micro-insurance plans

\- Automatic disruption detection

\- AI-assisted premium calculation

\- AI-based fraud detection

\- Automatic payout without manual claims



Instead of asking the worker to submit complex claims, the system automatically detects whether a disruption happened using trusted external data and sends the payout if the worker is eligible.



This makes the solution faster, simpler, fairer, more suitable for gig workers.





\## Why This Problem Matters



Gig workers depend on continuous daily activity to earn money. Even one or two bad days in a week can reduce their income significantly.



\### Key Challenges Faced by Gig Workers



\- No fixed salary

\- No paid leave

\- No protection during city-wide disruptions

\- Income can drop suddenly due to weather or public restrictions

\- Traditional insurance is too slow and not designed for short-term income loss



\### Why Parametric Insurance Works Here



Parametric insurance is ideal because:



\- It is based on predefined triggers

\- It does not require long claim verification

\- Payouts can be processed quickly

\- It is transparent and easy to understand

\### Why Weekly Premium Works Best

\- Better aligned with gig worker cash flow

\- Easier to understand

\- Flexible for different income levels

|---------------|----------------|------------------|------------|

\### How Premium is Calculated



The weekly premium is determined based on:



\- City-level disruption frequency

\- Historical weather patterns

\- Pollution levels

\- Local mobility restrictions

\- Platform activity volatility

\- Worker risk zone



\### Example



\- Worker in a low-risk area → lower premium

\- Worker in a flood-prone or high-pollution area → slightly higher premium



This keeps pricing fair and sustainable.







\## Parametric Trigger Design



A parametric insurance product needs clear, measurable triggers.



Instead of asking the worker to prove loss manually, the system uses trusted external data.



\### Proposed Trigger Categories



\#### A. Environmental Triggers

Used when weather or pollution directly affects work.



&#x20;----------------------------------------------------------

| Event 	   | Trigger Condition 			   |

|------------------|---------------------------------------|

| Heavy Rain 	   | Rainfall > 60 mm in 24 hours 	   |

| Heatwave 	   | Temperature > 45°C 		   |

| Flood Alert 	   | Official flood warning in worker zone |

| Severe Pollution | AQI > 350 				   |

&#x20;----------------------------------------------------------



\#### B. Mobility / Public Restriction Triggers

Used when workers cannot move or access delivery zones.



&#x20;----------------------------------------------------------------

| Event 	    | Trigger Condition 			 |

|-------------------|--------------------------------------------|

| Curfew 	    | Government-issued local curfew 		 |

| Strike / Shutdown | Official city or local strike alert 	 |

| Road Closure 	    | Major route closures affecting service area|

| Restricted Zone   | Local administration blocks movement 	 |

&#x20;----------------------------------------------------------------



\#### C. Platform Activity Triggers

Used when external conditions reduce work availability.



&#x20;--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

| Event 		 | Trigger Condition 					 |

|------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|

| Delivery Activity Drop | >50% drop in orders vs normal baseline 		 |

| Service Zone Shutdown  | Platform stops accepting orders in that area 	 |

| Rider Downtime Spike   | Unusual increase in worker idle time in affected zone |

&#x20;--------------------------------------------------------------------------------



\### Why This Trigger Design is Strong



\- It is measurable

\- It reduces manual disputes

\- It supports automation

\- It is easy for judges to understand

\- It matches real gig economy behavior

\## AI / ML Integration in the Workflow



The problem statement clearly asks for how AI/ML will be integrated.



We propose AI/ML in three main areas:



1\. Premium calculation

2\. Disruption prediction

3\. Fraud detection







\## AI Model 1 – Disruption Prediction Model



\### Purpose:

To predict whether a disruption is likely to affect a worker’s income in a specific location.



\### Inputs:



\- Rainfall data

\- Temperature

\- AQI levels

\- Flood alerts

\- Traffic / road restriction signals

\- Historical disruption patterns

\- Delivery activity trends in the zone



\### Model Options:

\- Random Forest

\- XGBoost

\- Gradient Boosting



\### Why This Model is Useful:

It helps the platform:



\- Detect high-risk situations early

\- Understand disruption severity

\- Improve payout confidence

\- Reduce false triggers



\### Output:

A Disruption Risk Score between 0 and 1



Example:



\- 0.2 → low risk

\- 0.6 → medium risk

\- 0.85 → high risk



If the risk crosses a threshold and trigger rules are met, payout processing begins.







\## AI Model 2 – Dynamic Premium Calculation



\### Purpose:

To calculate a fair weekly premium based on the worker’s risk environment.



\### Inputs:



\- Worker city

\- Historical weather volatility

\- Frequency of disruptions

\- Zone-level platform downtime

\- Pollution severity trends

\- Seasonal conditions



\### Model Options:



\- Linear Regression

\- Risk Scoring Engine

\- Rule + ML Hybrid Model



\### Why This Matters:



\- Keeps the product affordable

\- Prevents underpricing in risky zones

\- Prevents overpricing in safer zones

\- Makes pricing smarter over time



\### Output:

Recommended weekly premium for each worker or zone.







\## AI Model 3 – Fraud Detection Model



\### Purpose:

To prevent false or abusive payouts.



\### Fraud Risks We Consider:



\- Worker claims from an unaffected zone

\- Fake location or spoofed activity

\- Multiple suspicious claims in short time

\- Inactive workers trying to receive payouts

\- Repeated claims from abnormal behavior patterns



\### Inputs:



\- Worker’s registered work zone

\- Last active working sessions

\- Login timestamps

\- Time of policy activation

\- Event timing

\- Historical payout behavior

\- Repeated abnormal request patterns



\### Model Options:



\- Isolation Forest

\- Anomaly Detection

\- Rule-based risk engine + ML scoring



\### Why This Matters:

Fraud prevention is critical for insurance sustainability.



\### Output:

A Fraud Risk Score - 

If the score is too high, the payout is flagged for manual review or blocked.
